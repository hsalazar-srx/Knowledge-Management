## Context

Raised in the 2026-01-21 Melbourne visit DISP session with Devian. DISP (Defence Industry Security Program) is the Australian government framework (based on ISO 27005 and Essential Eight) that certifies suppliers for defence sector contracts.

SRX is pursuing DISP because:
- **Safran** is a prospect in the defence sector
- **John Ferrieks** has expressed intent to enter the defence market
- SRX has recently become a **Public Company** — increasing governance expectations
- DISP was identified as "the path to attract defence" contracts

A consultant (DiStefano, ~$24K) was proposed to support the gap analysis and guide the submission process. Devian recommended starting with Essential Eight, ISO 27005, internal training, and physical security as initial research areas before committing.

Karen agreed to perform the ICT perspective cost and gap analysis first.

## Implications

- ICT security gaps are currently significant — described as a legacy of the previous cost-saving-centric management style.
- Scanfil has online training tools (Dozier) that may fill some training and awareness gaps.
- Essential Eight compliance is a prerequisite component of DISP.
- Physical security and governance gaps need to be assessed alongside ICT security.
- Is Christian part of the DCN (Document Change Notification / approval body)? — open question for DISP approval processes.
- MES procedure expiry alignment (Mihai) is a parallel compliance task that may inform the DISP readiness picture.
