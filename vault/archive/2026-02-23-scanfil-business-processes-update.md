## Summary

Scanfil published an intranet announcement on **2026-02-23** describing a mandatory business process standardisation framework being rolled out across all Scanfil sites during its strategic growth phase.

**Key facts:**
- **38 processes** total; top **12 most critical** simplified and released in SMS by 2026-02-26
- **Remaining 26** to be released by H1 2026
- **SRX Global sites** (JB, Melbourne, Detroit, Cortona + 3 MB sites) are classified as **new Scanfil sites** (part of Scanfil as of 2024)
- **New sites must** implement the top 12 at the first phase of integration, or per local business plan
- **Spring 2026**: Scanfil-led in-depth process training specifically for SRXGlobal sites
- **Integration manager** is responsible for the new-site implementation roadmap

## Strategy Flag — New IT Compliance Obligation Not in Current Strategy

The IT portal strategy (`strategy/it-portal-priority-ordering-myinvois-then-ecn-then-other.md`) does not mention Scanfil group process compliance as an IT obligation. Three of the top 12 mandatory processes are IT-owned, with a spring 2026 training requirement. **This is not a contradiction** to the portal priority ordering, but it represents an **uncaptured parallel IT obligation** that will consume Karen's and the IT team's time.

The DISP project (`projects/disp-defence-readiness.md`) is now materially strengthened by this announcement: Scanfil group IT/IS Security Policy provides a ready-made baseline that reduces the cost and scope of the gap analysis.
